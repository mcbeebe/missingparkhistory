What This Is

This is an interactive map documenting 874+ historical signs and exhibits that have been flagged, revised, or removed from U.S. National Parks and Wildlife Refuges under Secretary's Order 3431 and Executive Order 14253.

Each entry on the map includes the park name, the specific interpretive material affected, the status of the directive (remove, revise, flagged for review, or court-restored), and — where available — photographs of the signs before and after censorship.

Executive Order 14253 “Restoring Truth and Sanity to American History” and Secretary’s Order 3431 directed parks to censor any content “that inappropriately disparages Americans…or that emphasizes matters unrelated to the beauty, abundance, or grandeur” of natural features.

Why It Matters

The censorship campaign targets history that this administration finds inconvenient: the stories of enslaved people at plantation sites, the science of climate change at glacier parks, the experiences of Japanese Americans at incarceration camps, and the Civil Rights movement at memorial sites across the country.

Without documentation, these removals happen silently. Most Americans will never know what they lost. This project exists to make the censorship visible, searchable, and impossible to ignore.

How It Was Built

This project is built on a leaked internal National Park Service database — the “Wayside Machine” — which catalogs every interpretive sign and exhibit across the park system. When Secretary’s Order 3431 was issued, park staff were directed to review and flag content for removal or revision.

We cross-referenced the leaked database with the Save Our Signs Tracker, Wayback Machine snapshots, FOIA requests, and on-the-ground photography from park visitors and journalists to build the most comprehensive public record of what’s being erased.

U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service entries are sourced from Sierra Club FOIA data — records obtained through Freedom of Information Act requests documenting interpretive exhibits flagged or removed at National Wildlife Refuges. This data supplements the NPS-focused Wayside Machine leak and extends our coverage to the broader federal public lands system.

Transparency matters. This project makes extraordinary claims backed by leaked government data. Every entry traces back to the internal NPS Wayside Machine database, cross-referenced with the SOS Tracker, Wayback Machine archives, and press reporting.
